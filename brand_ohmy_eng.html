OH, MY

Oh, my is a Russian brand of everyday clothing, created in St. Petersburg in 2008.

Today, the brand produces over 100 models of clothing from quality materials: dresses, skirts, sweaters, sweetshorts, hoodies, track pants, socks, etc.

Oh, my makes basic apparel. The colour palette is one of the distinguishing features of the company’s clothing models. They are all made in white, black and grey shades. Natural materials are used: cotton, wool and cashmere.

Such apparel with aboundance of decorative fixtures have turned out to be in high demand. Anyone wants to look simple, natural and neat. At the same time, these models match well with fashionable clothes from other brands.

During the first few years of its existence, the company developed through the efforts of three young specialists who believed in a higher goal: to create the No. 1 national Russian apparel brand. Since that time, the company has managed to organize production in Leningrad region, St. Petersburg and even Latvia.

Originally, the selection included the most basic models made from 100% cotton: dresses, t-shirts, pullovers, sweatshirts, skirts, undershirts. Every year new models and lines of clothing created on the basis of classic silhouettes have been added.

In April 2010, Oh, my opened its own online store and began working with wholesalers. Today, the online store (www.ohmyltd.ru) is the main sales channel, where the brand’s whole line is represented, including items released in limited quantities.

Oh, my!” is an expression of delight, positive emotion and joy, regardless of whether the phrase is uttered by a foreigner or a Russian. This is precisely the sort of feeling shoppers should experience when purchasing Oh, my clothes.

Audience

Demand for high quality basic clothing turned out to be very high among Russian consumers. It makes up more than half of all wardrobe items. Understanding this the team of Oh, my has focused on selecting high-quality fabrics that passed certification and control at the factories and on creating new fashions models of the most in-demand seasonal pieces. Despite the high demand and quality, the company retains a price policy that makes it possible for residents of the megalopolis as well as those in smaller towns around Russia to buy clothing under the Oh, my brand.

In 2013, production terms changed from 6 to 2 months.

The audience for the brand Oh, my on social networks is approximately 20,000 people.

Business profitability – around 15%

Sales dynamics over 3 years

Based on the results from 2012, Oh, my has become the best brand in the online vote of news&lifestyle resource The Village, overtaking all eminent competitors.

During the same year, the company set the goal of doubling its sales, reducing the duration of its production process and beginning work with hired designers from the world of fashion.

Thus, 2013 was a breakthrough year for the company: the timeframes for the development and production of models was decreased from six months to two; intrinsic costs fell by 20%, while sales increased 9-fold over 3 years (2.5-fold over the year). In the spring of the same year Oh, my developed a uniform for Ice-Cream Sellers in Gorky Park; the company took part in the Afisha Picnic Projects. In Autumn, it began the production of a collaborative line with the British brand ZDDZ.

The plans for 2014 include increasing production in Russia and abroad, increasing the volume of sales to one million dollars, perfecting the most in-demand models of clothing, using seasonal trends as a guide. In addition, the new collections will continue to be introduced in partnership with Russian and European designers.

Today, more than 10 people work at Oh, my, involved in the development of  the apparel, marketing and sales.

Sergey Kovelenov
Director, Oh, my
Birthday: 24 august 1984

Mr. Kovelenov was employed for several years at a number of leading publishing houses based in St. Petersburg, where he acquired extensive experience in the field of PR, marketing sales and management. His background in historical research during his university years helped him shape his life principles and future goals.

Working for the Sales Department of Ikea, Sergey also acquired practical knowledge in the field of design, which he subsequently used to create his future project.

The idea of producing simply-designed, high-quality clothes that would fit anyone well came to him quite naturally – there weren’t many manufacturers of simple, everyday clothes that people would want to wear and wear without taking off. The main goal was to determine the brand and its audience.

In 2008, Sergey created the Oh, my brand, which had previously been called Oh, my Posh and then renamed Oh, my. In 2010, Sergey opened an online company store (www.ohmyltd.ru), which today is the main venue for wholesale and retail of the brand’s entire product range in Russia.

In addition to fashion, Sergey studies photography. He loves to travel, seeking inspiration in various architectural urban ensembles and landscapes.
